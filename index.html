Welcome!

One cool thing about Tic Tac Toe is that it's one of the simplest yet most effective illustrations of the concept of a "zero-sum game" in game theory. In a zero-sum game, one player's gain is exactly balanced by the losses of the other player. For Tic Tac Toe, this means that every advantage one player gains directly results in a disadvantage for the other player.

Additionally, Tic Tac Toe can always end in a draw with optimal play from both players. The game has been fully solved, meaning there is a known, mathematically optimal way to play for both the starting player (X) and the second player (O). If both players play optimally, the game will always result in a tie. This property makes it a fascinating study in strategy and game theory despite its apparent simplicity.

Player to Player Tic Tac Toe Rules

Game Set-up:

  1. Board: Decide who will go first. The first player will be X and the second player will be O.
  2. Players: There are two players, Player 1 is "X" and Player 2 is "O".

How to Play:

  1. Starting the Game: The game is played on a 3x3 grid.
  2. Taking Turns: Players take turns placing their mark (X or O) in an empty cell on the grid. Player 1 (X) goes first, followed by Player 2 (O).
  3. Marking the Grid: Click or tap on an empty cell to place your mark. Once placed, the mark cannot be moved or removed.

Winning the Game:

  1. Winning Condition: A player wins if they are the first to get three of their marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).
  2. Draw: If all nine cells are filled and neither player has three in a row, the game ends in a draw.

Ending the Game:

  1. Declare Winner: Once a player has three marks in a row, the round ends, and that player is declared the winner of the round. The first player to win 3 rounds has won the game.
  2. Declare Draw: If all cells are filled without a winner, declare the game a draw.

Optional Rules:

  1. Rematch: After the game ends, players can choose to play again, switching who goes first.
